The G.O.A.T's top 10

Not ranked in any order

Site Name Site URL Description
Windows 93 https://www.windows93.net A warped parody of a Windows OS. Play fake games, open glitchy apps, and explore a surreal 90s tech dreamscape.
Zoom Quilt https://zoomquilt.org/ An infinite zooming patchwork of surreal art. Use arrow keys to control speed. Hypnotic and collaborative, from the early 2000s.
Space Jam 1996 https://www.spacejam.com/1996/ The original movie site, frozen in time with GeoCities-style design. A gem of 90s web nostalgia.
Million Dollar Homepage http://www.milliondollarhomepage.com/ A 2005 experiment where pixels were sold for $1 each. Now a chaotic mosaic of tiny ads and links, many still live. Go at your own risk.
Drive Me Insane https://www.drivemeinsane.com/ Since 1997, you've been able to have some fun at another person's expense with this fun website! Waste time turning on and off the lights in their office, play with a disco ball and even display your own message on their computer!
Zombo.com http://zombo.com Endless looping voice welcomes you to "infinite possibilities" with trippy animations. A pointless classic from the early internet.
The Useless web https://theuselessweb.com/ One button takes you to random, bizarre sites—like bouncing cats or pointless games. A rabbit hole of oddities. Make sure to keep trying it to get endless brainrot.
The Official Ninja Homepage https://www.realultimatepower.net/ A satirical early-2000s site worshipping ninjas with over-the-top text and flip-kick facts. It's got that raw, unhinged HTML charm.
Fake Windows Update Screen https://hackertyper.net/ Smash your keyboard and watch fake code scroll like you're a Hollywood hacker. It's all for show—zero skill needed, just pure retro aesthetic from 2011.
This cell is aligned to the top. https://www.spaghettimonster.org/ The satirical “religion” worshiping a pasta deity, with a site full of faux-serious scriptures and goofy graphics. It's got that early-2000s internet humor vibe.
